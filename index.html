Welcome to TransitHack: Revolutionizing Baku's Public Transport!

Are you passionate about improving urban mobility? Join us for TransitHack, a groundbreaking hackathon focused on transforming Baku's public transportation system!

Event Overview

TransitHack is a unique 48-hour event where participants will work on innovative solutions to enhance public transport coverage, efficiency, and attractiveness in Baku. Our goal? To reduce car usage and decrease traffic congestion in our beautiful city.

What makes TransitHack special?

  1. Research & Development Focus: We prioritize solving real urban challenges over creating business models. Your ideas don't need to be the next unicorn startup - they just need to make a positive impact on Baku's transportation system.
  2. Interdisciplinary Approach: This isn't just a coding event! We welcome participants from various backgrounds, including:
    • Software development
    • Urban planning
    • Civil engineering
    • Mathematics
    • Economics
    • Public policy
    • And more!
  3. Real-World Impact: Your projects will be judged based on their potential to improve public utility, value to citizens, innovation, and feasibility.

Project Ideas

Need inspiration? Here are some potential project areas:

  1. On-Demand Public Transport: Design a system for dynamic minibus routes based on real-time passenger demand.
  2. Public Transport Coverage Heatmap: Create an algorithm to visualize and quantify the quality of public transport connections across Baku.
  3. Extended Service Hours Analysis: Conduct a cost-benefit analysis of extending public transport operating hours.
  4. Traffic Flow Optimization: Develop solutions to redistribute traffic from major avenues to smaller streets, improving overall city mobility.
  5. Regional Rail Network Planning: Propose an optimal railway route for the greater Baku area, including cost-benefit analysis.

What to Expect

  • Duration: 48 hours of intense collaboration and innovation
  • Team Formation: Form teams of 3-5 people or join as an individual and meet new teammates
  • Mentorship: Access to expert mentors in urban planning, technology, and transportation
  • Workshops: Skill-building sessions on relevant tools and methodologies
  • Judging: Present your project to a panel of esteemed experts in the field

Judging Criteria

Your projects will be evaluated based on:

  • Public utility and value to citizens
  • Innovation and creativity
  • Technical feasibility
  • Quality of research and analysis
  • Potential for real-world implementation

Note: We focus on the quality of your ideas and solutions, not on polished presentations or business pitches.
